Three Nights in the Woods

  • Originality:

    I do realize the game shares some (unintentional) similarities with games like Don't Starve and Slender: The Eight Pages (which I have not played) mostly that it is a survival game with horror elements.

    However, I do believe my "stealth" mechanic makes it somewhat unique.

    The rather quick pace of gameplay and short nature also makes it stand out somewhat.

  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    • For this game I used a free program called Tiled to create the tilemaps and generate a tilemap JSON for phaser.
    • I think I'm getting better at setting up maps.
    • This is the first game I've made with multiple turns and multiple lose conditions.
    • The game also has simple primary and secondary goals.
    • Unlike my other games the player has more than a few elements to keep track of.
    • Also unlike my other games there is a simplistic ramping difficulty.
    • Simple "stealth" mechanic
    • I like the animated menu screen.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    • The easiest (and most fun) part was creating the assets.
    • The hardest part was time management (I think I spent too much time on creating the assets).
    • I'm still getting used to Phaser.
  • Prototype Assets: Did you make your prototype assets from scratch? Did you borrow them? Cite your sources here.
